How much will it cost me?

The price I might charge you is not the only cost you may be faced with during this venture. Before you decide on getting a website, you should know what costs to expect. There are 3 main areas of a website which can take up money.

Design, Development and Maintenace

This is the costs of having someone make and maintain the website for you. This is what I do and whether I charge you depends on what you order. Once the website is made, you may want to modify some content. There are 2 options here. You can contact me and I will change the content for you (within a day or two) or I can write a simple Content Management System (CMS) for you. A CMS allows you to log in as an admin and edit the content on your site. Setting up a CMS can be a lot of work, but once set up, it gives you full control over your content. You will still have to contact me if you want to change more complex parts of your website.

Hosting

A host will keep your website on a server so people can see it. Hosting prices can range from 0 to Â£1000 a month. This choice depends greatly on your website. If you expect a lot of traffic you will want a host that offers unlimited bandwidth and is very reliable. In such case you may consider paying a little for a better server. If however you are an artist and the website is a portfolio that is only seen now and again, then you can probably manage going for a free host. This website is hosted at a host called Heroku and I'm not paying anything for it. As you can see the website is still fast and works perfectly despite the fact that I'm hosting it for free.

Different hosts support different kinds of websites. Ask me what are the requirements (usually Django) of your website before looking for a host. Otherwise, the host you find may not support your website.

Domain Name Registration

This step is not required as hosts will provide you with a sub-domain. A sub-domain is a half yours half the hosts domain. For example instead of having your own domain: www.something.com, you will have: www.something.something.com. You may purchase a domain at any time so do not have to worry about it that much. In general domains cost between £5 and £15 a year with £15 being for the popular ones like .com. Domain registration is very simple, you simply only provide the name of your website and what you want the domain to be. I will help you with this up if you ask.

This website has a domain, dackowebdesign.co.uk. However if I hadn't purchased this domain, the url would be dackowebdesign.herokuapp.com (this still works, only no one uses it)
